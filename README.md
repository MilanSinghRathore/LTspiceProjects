#  Cryo-CMOS Characteristics Study using LTspice

## 🔹 Overview

This project focuses on the analysis of CMOS device characteristics at cryogenic temperatures using LTspice. The study investigates how transistor behavior changes at low temperatures compared to room temperature, which is important for emerging applications such as quantum computing and low-power electronics.

---

## 🔹 Objectives

* Analyze CMOS behavior at cryogenic temperatures
* Compare performance with room temperature (300K)
* Study variation in key electrical parameters
* Evaluate suitability for advanced electronic systems

---

## 🔹 Tools Used

* LTspice
* CMOS Device Models

---

## 🔹 Parameters Analyzed

* Threshold Voltage (Vth)
* Drain Current (Id)
* Leakage Current
* Transconductance (gm)
* Subthreshold Slope

---

## 🔹 Methodology

1. CMOS circuits are designed and simulated in LTspice
2. Temperature is varied (e.g., 300K → 77K → 4K)
3. Electrical characteristics are extracted
4. Results are compared across temperature ranges

---

## 🔹 Results & Observations

* Increased carrier mobility at low temperatures
* Reduced leakage current
* Shift in threshold voltage
* Improved switching characteristics in some cases



---

## 🔹 Applications

* Quantum Computing Interfaces
* Cryogenic Memory Systems
* Space Electronics
* Low-Power VLSI Design
